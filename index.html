What is a Vivarium?

A vivarium is an enclosed space for keeping and observing animals or plants, often for scientific research or as a pet. The word vivarium comes from the Latin word vivus, which means "alive".

Vivariums can be used to simulate a portion of an ecosystem for a particular species, with controlled environmental conditions like temperature, humidity, and light. They can be as small as a corked bottle or as large as 600 gallons.
